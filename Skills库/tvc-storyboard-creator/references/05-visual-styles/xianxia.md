# Xianxia (仙侠) Fantasy Style Guide

## Overview

Xianxia (仙侠) is a Chinese fantasy genre featuring immortal cultivators, magical realms, and ancient Chinese aesthetics. This style combines martial arts with fantasy elements, creating visually stunning imagery rooted in Chinese mythology.

---

## Visual Characteristics

### Color Palette

**Primary Tones**:
- Mystical whites (#f8f8ff)
- Ethereal blues (#1e3a5f)
- Jade greens (#00a86b)
- Gold accents (#ffd700)
- Blood reds (#8b0000)

**Atmospheric Colors**:
- Misty whites/greys
- Purple mysticism
- Silver moonlight
- Fire reds

### Elements

**Clothing**:
- Flowing robes (Hanfu/traditional)
- Long, billowing sleeves
- Layered garments
- Sash/ribbon details
- Ancient accessories

**Props**:
- Swords/weapons (flying swords)
- Mirrors/talismans
- Jade ornaments
- Lanterns
- scrolls

## Cultural Notes

### Meaning

- **仙 (Xian)**: Immortal/cultivator
- **侠 (Xia)**: Chivalrous warrior

### Values

- Cultivation/self-improvement
- Righteousness
- Loyalty
- Honor

### Avoid

- Religious specificity
- Political references
- Sensitive symbolism

---

## Common Mistakes

### Avoid

1. **Modern elements**: Breaks fantasy
2. **Korean/Japanese mix**: Authentic Chinese
3. **Cluttered scenes**: Clear composition
4. **Flat colors**: Atmospheric depth
5. **Wrong symbolism**: Research needed

---

## Mixing Styles

### Compatible Combinations

- **+ Wes Anderson**: Symmetrical xianxia
- **+ Disney**: Magical transformation
- **+ Traditional Chinese**: Heritage emphasis

### Challenging Combinations

- **+ Cyberpunk**: Complete contrast
- **+ Gritty realism**: Tone clash
