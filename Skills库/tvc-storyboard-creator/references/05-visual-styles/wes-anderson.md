# Wes Anderson Style Guide

## Overview

Wes Anderson is one of cinema's most recognizable visual directors, known for his distinctive symmetrical compositions, pastel color palettes, and whimsical storytelling. This style has become a cultural phenomenon, influencing advertising and visual content globally.

---

## Visual Characteristics

### Color Palette

**Primary Colors**:
- Pastel pinks (#FFB7B2)
- Soft yellows (#FFE066)
- Mint greens (#B5EAD7)
- Baby blues (#A2D2FF)
- Lavender (#E2F0CB → #C7CEEA)

**Tonal Approach**:
- Muted, desaturated tones
- Warm undertones
- Consistent color temperature
- Color harmony within frames

### Composition

**Symmetry**:
- Perfect center composition
- Two-point symmetry
- Balanced framing
- Grid-like structure

**Framing**:
- Flat, frontal angles
- Fixed camera positions
- Minimal depth
- Poster-like layouts

**Negative Space**:
- Used intentionally
- Creates breathing room
- Draws focus to subject

### Set Design

**Elements**:
- Vintage props
- Patterned wallpapers
- Collectible objects
- Symmetrical arrangements
- Retro aesthetic

**Miniatures**:
- Tilted miniature effect
- Forced perspective
- Toy-like quality

### Character Direction

**Acting Style**:
- Deadpan delivery
- Matter-of-fact expressions
- Quirky behaviors
- Costume coordination

---

## Shot Types

### Common Shots

| Shot Type | Characteristics | Usage |
|-----------|----------------|-------|
| Front-facing | Camera directly in front | Establish symmetry |
| Two-shot | Characters side by side | Balanced composition |
| Over-shoulder | Through objects framing | Depth creation |
| High angle | Looking down | Miniature effect |
| Eye-level | Neutral | Character focus |

### Movement

- **Static preferred**: Fixed camera positions
- **Slow pans**: Gentle horizontal movement
- **Tilt**: Vertical camera movement
- **Tracking**: Slow lateral movement

---

## Lighting

### Key Characteristics

1. **Soft, Even Light**
   - Diffused light sources
   - Minimal shadows
   - Flat lighting

2. **Practical Lights**
   - Visible light sources
   - Colored gels
   - Warm tones

3. **Backlighting**
   - Rim light on subjects
   - Silhouette creation

---

## Film References

### Key Films

- **The Grand Budapest Hotel**: Pink/red tones, elaborate sets
- **Moonrise Kingdom**: Yellows, nature, childhood
- **The Royal Tenenbaums**: Muted, vintage, family
- **Rushmore**: School setting, primary colors
- **Isle of Dogs**: Muted palette, Japanese influence

## Common Mistakes

### Avoid

1. **Over-symmetry**: Can feel robotic
2. **Color overload**: Too many pastels
3. **Forced quirkiness**: Must feel natural
4. **Modern elements**: Breaks immersion
5. **Dark tones**: Contravenes style

---

## Mixing Styles

### Compatible Combinations

- **+ Disney**: Whimsical magic
- **+ Minimalist**: Clean symmetry
- **+ Vintage**: Strong retro feel

### Challenging Combinations

- **+ Cyberpunk**: Tone conflict
- **+ Gritty**: Too much contrast
